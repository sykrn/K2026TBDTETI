Vision & Mission

Curriculum foundation prioritizing innovation for the nation's interests

Faculty of Engineering Vision

UGM Faculty of Engineering aims to be an engineering higher education institution with national and global networks to strengthen the nation's independence and sovereignty in science and technology, slowing down global entropy, and building a new civilization.

DTETI Department Vision

To become a universal source of innovation in Electrical Engineering & Information Technology for the benefit of the nation and humanity, inspired by national cultural values based on Pancasila.

Study Program Scientific Vision

To become a universal source of innovation in Electrical Engineering & Information Technology in solving Biomedical problems for the benefit of the nation and humanity.

PEO & SO

Program Educational Objectives & Student Outcomes

PEO 1 — Character

Demonstrate strong leadership qualities, uphold ethical standards, and pursue lifelong learning with adaptability to engage in global and social issues.

PEO 2 — Career

Achieve success in a technical or professional career in biomedical engineering by demonstrating integrity, professional competence, and effective communication.

Student Outcomes (SO)

1
Uphold Ethics and Professional Responsibilities

Recognize professional responsibilities, act with moral integrity, and make decisions based on ethical, legal, and social impact considerations.

2
Apply Problem-Solving and Experimentation Skills

Analyze complex biomedical engineering problems by applying mathematical, biological, physical, and engineering principles.

3
Design and Implement Contextual Engineering Solutions

Design, implement, and evaluate engineering solutions that meet specific user needs while considering realistic constraints.

4
Communicate and Collaborate Effectively

Communicate effectively in various professional formats and function as a member or leader of a multidisciplinary team.

5
Engage in Lifelong Learning and Self-Development

Acquire and apply new knowledge, master modern engineering tools, and demonstrate a commitment to continuous self-development.

Curriculum Transition

Transition guide to the 2026 Curriculum

Four transition principles apply to all cohorts (2023–2025).

Credit Recognition

Passed courses are recognized and equivalentized with corresponding courses in the new structure.

Additional Courses

New mandatory courses not present in the old curriculum must be taken as additions.

Automatic Replacement

Some old courses are directly replaced by corresponding new courses.

Adjusted Workload

Total graduation credits are adjusted per cohort so that the study period remains efficient.

Graduation

2026 Curriculum Graduation Requirements

The following are the graduation criteria and requirements for undergraduate students of the Biomedical Engineering Program DTETI UGM.

26

Have achieved all Student Outcomes (SO/CPL).

27

Have completed and passed the courses Practicum, Individual Project, Biomedical Engineering Design Project 1, and Biomedical Engineering Design Project 2 according to PSPSTB provisions.

28

Obtain a minimum grade of C for the courses Pancasila, Religion, and Civics / Citizenship.

29

Have passed all mandatory courses and elective courses required in the 2026 Curriculum with the following provisions:

  • a. Minimum total of 144 Credits
  • b. Minimum Grade Point Average (GPA) of 2.00
  • c. Total credits with a D grade maximum of 25% (equivalent to 36 credits) and no E grades
30

Graduation grade requirements for university and faculty level courses follow the general academic guidelines set by the university and faculty.

31

Have English proficiency TOEFL (Institutional Testing Program or International) or similar, evidenced by one of the following scores:

AcEPT UGM ≥ 175
TOEFL ITP ≥ 450
TOEFL iBT ≥ 54
IELTS ≥ 5.0
TOEIC ≥ 450
Duolingo ≥ 70
TOEFL-like (PBT) ≥ 450
